# Trailhead

## One-liner
A community app where local hikers post and read up-to-the-day trail condition reports, so nobody drives an hour to a washed-out or snowed-in trail.

## The problem
Hikers, trail runners, and casual walkers plan outings off stale information — official park pages update weekly at best, and a trail that was clear on Monday can be a mudslide by Saturday. The pain is wasted trips, unsafe surprises (ice, downed trees, flooded crossings), and turning back at the trailhead. It matters most to people who travel 30+ minutes to reach a trail.

## The value
Before: you guess from a week-old park page or a stranger's year-old blog post. After: you open Trailhead, see "reported 3 hours ago: creek crossing knee-deep, passable with poles," and decide with confidence. Regular hikers get a quick way to give back by logging what they saw on the way out.

## Product category
Community content product (user-generated reports) with a curated editorial layer (featured trails maintained by local moderators).

## What success looks like
A year in, on a Friday night the local hiking Facebook group has mostly stopped asking "anyone been to Eagle Ridge lately?" because the answer is already on Trailhead. Each popular trail has a fresh report within the last day or two, moderators have cleaned up duplicate trails, and a handful of "trusted reporters" reliably post after their hikes.
